# Key Business Findings

This document summarizes the key insights derived from the SQL-based business performance
and customer behavior analysis.

---

## 1. Revenue Performance

- The business generates consistent revenue across months, with visible month-over-month
  fluctuations indicating potential seasonality.
- A small subset of months contributes disproportionately to total annual revenue,
  suggesting opportunities for targeted marketing during high-performing periods.

---

## 2. Customer Behavior Insights

- Repeat customers contribute significantly more revenue compared to one-time customers,
  highlighting the importance of customer retention strategies.
- Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) analysis shows that a small group of high-value customers
  drives a large portion of overall revenue.
- Customer segmentation reveals three clear groups: VIP, Regular, and Low-Value customers,
  which can be leveraged for personalized marketing campaigns.

---

## 3. Product Performance Insights

- Product-level analysis indicates that a limited number of products generate the majority
  of total revenue, following the Pareto (80/20) principle.
- Best-selling products by quantity are not always the highest revenue generators,
  suggesting differences in pricing strategies across products.
- Certain product categories consistently outperform others in both revenue and sales volume.

---

## 4. Category-Level Analysis

- High-performing categories contribute a significant percentage of total revenue despite
  having fewer products, indicating strong product-market fit.
- Lower-performing categories may require pricing optimization or promotional strategies
  to improve performance.

---

## 5. Business Recommendations

- Focus marketing and inventory efforts on top-performing products contributing to the
  first 80% of revenue.
- Implement loyalty programs to improve repeat customer rate and maximize customer lifetime value.
- Use category performance insights to guide future product expansion and optimization.
